Methodology

A cross-sectional survey research design was employed in this study. The participants consisted of 336 medical students from King Saud University selected through stratified sampling. Data were gathered using a structured questionnaire that measured socio-demographic characteristics, personal traits, and study habit patterns. Academic achievement was assessed using the studentsâ€™ grade point averages (GPA). The collected data were analyzed using descriptive statistics and inferential statistical tests to determine the relationship between study habits, personal factors, and academic performance.
