Methodology

The research utilized a quantitative cross-sectional research design. A total of 1,016 college students participated in the study through an online survey. Standardized and validated instruments were used, including a time management behavior scale, a self-control scale, a mobile phone dependence scale, and a study engagement questionnaire. Data were analyzed using descriptive statistics to summarize participant responses, Pearson correlation analysis to identify relationships among variables, and mediation analysis using bootstrapping methods to examine indirect effects. Statistical analysis was conducted using specialized software to ensure accuracy and reliability of results.
