Statement of the Problem

  1. Salinity stress is increasingly affecting agricultural lands worldwide, yet there is insufficient comprehensive synthesis of how tomato plants are affected by salinity at multiple biological levels.
  2. Although individual studies have investigated specific traits or responses, there is a lack of consolidated knowledge on how salinity alters growth, physiology, biochemistry, yield, fruit quality, and gene expression in tomato plants.
  3. Because salinity stress is expected to expand globally, farmers and researchers need clearer evidence and recommendations to develop cropping practices and breeding strategies that improve tomato tolerance to salinity stress.
